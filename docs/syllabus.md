# Quantitative Methods for Social Sciences

## **12-Week Intensive Study Plan**

---

## Week 1: Introduction & Foundations of Quantitative Social Science

**Topics:**  

- Role of quantitative methods in social sciences  
- Measurement, variables, and data types  
- Overview of Python and R environments  
- Ethical considerations in quantitative research  

**Reading:**  

- King, Keohane & Verba (1994), *Designing Social Inquiry*, Chapters 1-2  
- Shadish, Cook & Campbell (2002), *Experimental and Quasi-Experimental Designs*, Chapter 1  
- Gelman et al. (2013), *Bayesian Data Analysis*, Introduction (optional)

---

## Week 2: Descriptive Statistics & Data Visualization

**Topics:**  

- Central tendency, variability, distributions  
- Data summarization techniques  
- Visualizing data: histograms, boxplots, scatterplots  
- Introduction to data wrangling in Python (pandas) and R (dplyr)  

**Reading:**  

- Tufte (2001), *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*, Chapters 1-3  
- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapters 1-2  
- Python pandas documentation (selected sections)  
- R for Data Science (Wickham & Grolemund), Chapters on data visualization (optional)

---

## Week 3: Probability & Statistical Inference Basics

**Topics:**  

- Probability theory essentials  
- Sampling and sampling distributions  
- Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing  
- p-values and statistical significance  

**Reading:**  

- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapters 3-4  
- King et al. (1994), Chapter 3  
- Shadish et al. (2002), Chapters 2-3  

---

## Week 4: Linear Regression & Model Interpretation

**Topics:**  

- Simple and multiple linear regression  
- Assumptions, diagnostics, and model fit  
- Interpreting coefficients and p-values  
- Introduction to causal inference concepts  

**Reading:**  

- Angrist & Pischke (2009), Chapters 2-3  
- Wooldridge (2010), Chapters 2-3  
- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapter 5  

---

## Week 5: Generalized Linear Models & Logistic Regression

**Topics:**  

- Logistic regression for binary outcomes  
- Model evaluation (ROC, confusion matrix)  
- Extensions: Poisson regression and count data  
- Practical applications in social research  

**Reading:**  

- Agresti, A. (2018), *An Introduction to Categorical Data Analysis*, Chapters 1-4 (optional)  
- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapter 6  
- Angrist & Pischke (2009), Chapter 4  

---

## Week 6: Bayesian Methods I — Foundations

**Topics:**  

- Bayesian vs frequentist paradigms  
- Bayes' theorem and updating beliefs  
- Prior, likelihood, posterior distributions  
- Simple Bayesian models in R using `brms`  

**Reading:**  

- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapters 1 & 7-8  
- McElreath (2020), Chapters 1-3  
- Imbens & Rubin (2015), Introduction to Bayesian inference  

---

## Week 7: Bayesian Methods II — Advanced Modeling

**Topics:**  

- Hierarchical/multilevel models  
- Model comparison and checking  
- Practical applications to social data  
- Introduction to Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)  

**Reading:**  

- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapters 12-14  
- McElreath (2020), Chapters 4-6  
- Imbens & Rubin (2015), Chapters on hierarchical models  

---

## Week 8: Matching & Causal Inference Techniques

**Topics:**  

- Potential outcomes framework  
- Propensity score matching  
- Difference-in-differences (DiD)  
- Instrumental variables overview  

**Reading:**  

- Angrist & Pischke (2009), Chapters 5-7  
- Imbens & Rubin (2015), Chapters 2-5  
- Shadish et al. (2002), Chapters 4-5  

---

## Week 9: Time Series & Panel Data Analysis

**Topics:**  

- Basics of time series data and autocorrelation  
- Fixed and random effects models  
- Dynamic panel data models  
- Applications in political and economic data  

**Reading:**  

- Wooldridge (2010), Chapters 10-12  
- Angrist & Pischke (2009), Chapter 8 (panel data)  
- King et al. (1994), sections on longitudinal data (optional)  

---

## Week 10: Text as Data & Computational Social Science

**Topics:**  

- Introduction to text mining and NLP basics  
- Sentiment analysis and topic modeling  
- Social media data and network analysis overview  
- Ethical considerations in computational methods  

**Reading:**  

- Grimmer & Stewart (2013), *Text as Data*  
- Blei, Ng & Jordan (2003), *Latent Dirichlet Allocation*  
- King et al. (1994), section on content analysis (optional)  

---

## Week 11: Model Validation & Robustness Checks

**Topics:**  

- Cross-validation and out-of-sample prediction  
- Sensitivity analyses  
- Addressing missing data and measurement error  
- Reproducibility and transparency in analysis  

**Reading:**  

- Gelman et al. (2013), Chapter 6 (model checking)  
- Angrist & Pischke (2009), Chapter 9 (robustness)  
- King et al. (1994), chapter on threats to validity  

---

## Week 12: Capstone Project & Presentation

**Topics:**  

- Integration of methods in a substantive research question  
- Data preparation and analysis  
- Writing up results and presenting findings  
- Peer feedback and iteration  

**Reading:**  

- Review previous readings as needed  
- Selected topical papers related to student projects  

---
